Modern & Historical Flat Design Examples

A look at how the principles of simple geometry and bold color have been applied over time.

Microsoft Metro UI (2010)

Microsoft's design language, used in Windows Phone and Windows 8, was the purest expression of early digital flat design. It prioritized typography and large, solid-color blocks (tiles) over ornamental graphics.

iOS 7 (2013)

Apple's dramatic shift away from skeuomorphism defined the second wave of flat design. It introduced thin line icons, blurred transparency effects, and a layered approach to depth rather than shadows.

Google Material Design (2014)

While not strictly 'flat,' Material Design incorporated flat elements (bold color, clean typography) but added a subtle layer system (using Z-axis shadows) to provide clarity on interaction and hierarchy.
